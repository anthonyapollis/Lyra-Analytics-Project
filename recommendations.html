01
Critical Priority   Clinical Operations · Risk Management

Deploy ML Risk Classifier in Real-Time Triage Workflow

The Risk Level Classifier (Random Forest, 95.2% accuracy) is trained and validated. Integrating it into the counsellor intake workflow enables automatic flagging of High and Critical risk sessions before the counsellor answers the call. With 46.4% of sessions presenting High/Critical risk, manual triage is a bottleneck — automation reduces response latency from 4.2 min to under 45 seconds.

02
Critical Priority   Crisis Services · Resource Allocation

Increase Critical Incident Response Centre Capacity by 40%

Crisis sessions (Trauma + Critical Incident Trauma) account for 6.4% of volume but generate 100% of emergency escalations. The 66 Critical Incident Response Centres are operating at over 90% utilisation during peak hours (18:00–21:00 SAST). A 40% capacity increase — achievable through hybrid counsellor allocation — would eliminate the current 8-minute average wait time for crisis callers.

03
High Priority   Digital Transformation · Utilisation

Accelerate Digital Care Hub Rollout — Target 35% of Sessions

Digital Care Hubs handle 28% of sessions globally but clients on Digital First packages show 34% higher utilisation rates. The gap reveals unmet demand — employees prefer digital-first engagement but infrastructure lags. Expanding Digital Care Hubs from 154 to 220 globally, with a focus on Gauteng and KZN, captures this demand while reducing cost-per-session by an estimated R1,200.

High Priority   SCD2 Analytics · HR Insights

Surface SCD2 Department-Change Burnout Signal to HR Partners

DimEmployee_SCD2 analysis reveals employees who changed department within 12 months have a 23% higher burnout presentation rate. This signal is invisible without SCD2 history tracking and is not being surfaced to HR partners in current reporting. Building a "workforce transition risk" dashboard delivers a unique early-warning capability that differentiates Lyra's analytics offering from competitors.

+23% burnout risk post-department-change · SCD2-exclusive insight

High Priority   Resolution · Follow-Up Automation

Automate Follow-Up Scheduling for Predicted Unresolved Cases

The Case Resolution Predictor (AUC 0.631) identifies sessions unlikely to close within a single encounter. Currently, 34.2% of sessions require follow-up but scheduling is manual — leading to 18% of high-risk cases waiting more than 5 days. Auto-scheduling follow-up within 48 hours for predicted-unresolved cases reduces this gap and improves outcome rates.

Medium Priority   Client Retention · Commercial

Introduce Utilisation-Linked Pricing for Enterprise EWP Clients

Enterprise EWP clients show utilisation rates ranging from 12% to 67% — a 5.5× spread. Clients below 20% utilisation are at elevated churn risk as they perceive low ROI on their contract. Restructuring pricing to reward utilisation above 40% with service credits creates an incentive alignment that increases both retention and session volume.

Medium Priority   Compliance · Data Governance

Build Jurisdiction-Aware Data Residency Enforcement

DimFacilityGeography already tags each facility with its PrivacyRegime (POPIA, HIPAA, PIPEDA, GDPR, Privacy Act). However, session data currently flows through a single Snowflake account. Implementing row-level security policies keyed on CountryCode and PrivacyRegime ensures that HIPAA data never enters POPIA jurisdiction processing — a requirement for US healthcare clients expanding into SA.

Medium Priority   Forecasting · Capacity Planning

Build Seasonal Session Demand Forecasting on DimDate Spine

The 10-year DimDate spine (2018–2028) enables time-series analysis of session demand patterns. Analysis reveals clear seasonality: January (return-from-leave anxiety), May–June (financial year-end stress), and October (year-end performance pressure) are peak demand months. A 12-week forward demand forecast enables proactive counsellor rostering and reduces session wait times during peaks.
